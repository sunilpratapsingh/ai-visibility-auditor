## Architecture: 6 Layers, 15 Categories

### LAYER 1: CAN AI FIND YOU? (28% weight)
1. AI Crawler Access (15%) — 20 crawlers, training/search, crawl-delay, meta robots
2. AI Discovery Files (8%) — llms.txt validated, NDJSON, .well-known
3. Sitemap & Indexing (5%) — multi-format, freshness, cross-reference

### LAYER 2: CAN AI UNDERSTAND YOU? (27%)
4. Schema & Structured Data (16%) — 30-type validation, completeness %, Speakable
5. Content Structure (7%) — heading tree, FAQ patterns, lists/tables, meta tags
6. Content Readability (4%) — ARI, passive voice, sentence length, vocabulary

### LAYER 3: WILL AI TRUST YOU? (18%)
7. Entity & Authority (8%) — Open Graph, sameAs, canonical, social, trust pages
8. E-E-A-T Signals (10%) — page-type adaptive, YMYL, author, credentials, citations

### LAYER 4: WILL AI QUOTE YOU? (15%)
9. Content Citeability (10%) — answer capsules, stat attribution, fact density, definitions
10. Promotional Tone (5%) — brand ratio, superlatives, CTA density, benefit-fact ratio

### LAYER 5: CAN AI RENDER YOU? (7% + info)
11. Technical AI-Readiness (7%) — semantic HTML, alt text, links, word count
12. Agent vs Human View (info) — content visibility %, JS-dependent detection
13. Page Performance (info) — DOM load, nodes, render-blocking

### LAYER 6: TRACK & EXPORT (utility)
14. Scan History & Trends — last 10 per domain, deltas, sparkline
15. Export & Reporting — markdown, JSON, CSV, HTML, quick links

## Promotional Tone Detector (5 Signals)

Based on Semrush study: promotional tone = -26.19% AI citation correlation.

Signal 1: Brand-centric language ratio (our/we/my + service terms ÷ total sentences)
Signal 2: Unsupported superlative claims (not followed by source citation)
Signal 3: CTA density (CTAs per 1000 words)
Signal 4: Benefit-over-fact ratio (benefit patterns ÷ fact patterns)
Signal 5: Comparative self-promotion ("unlike others", "the only")

Score: Start at 100, deduct per signal. 80-100 = non-promotional, 50-79 = mixed, 0-49 = promotional.

## Schema 3-Tier Validation

Tier 1 (Deep, ~30 types): Validate required + recommended properties per Google docs
Tier 2 (Basic): Any schema.org type — confirm valid JSON + has @type + name
Tier 3 (Unknown/Custom): Detect and report, no validation, no score impact
